An expedition to observe the rich flora and fauna in their natural habitat is an extraordinary treat that no nature and wildlife enthusiast would like to miss. The TIGER SAFARI, in particular needs expertise and understanding of the beautiful and majestic beast as the Tiger remains an enigma that centuries have failed to unfold.

 

Being the national animal of India, the TIGER is the representation of India’s wilderness. His supple majesty, substantial strength and calm gaze acts as a tranquilizer to the tantalizing wishes of all those who desire to capture this gargantuan lovely beast on their lenses. The Tiger is most recognizable, for its distinctive and dark vertical black stripes that are unique in each individual on reddish-orange fur with a lighter under side, and is the most popular of the world’s charismatic mega fauna. There is a notable sexual dimorphism between males and females, with the latter being consistently smaller than the males. Unlike most other cats, Tigers are great swimmers and actually like the water. Adult Tigers lead largely solitary lives except when females are raising cubs. Since the early 20th century, Tiger populations have lost at least 93% of their historic range. The Tiger is thus listed as Endangered on the IUCN Red List since 1986. The heaviest wild Tiger on record was a Bengal Tiger from North India which was shot in 1967.

 

India has got a rich history when it comes to Tigers and wildlife. Jim Corbett, the British hunter, tracker, naturalist and author who hunted a number of man-eating Tigers and leopards in India spoke out for the need to protect India’s Wildlife from extermination. Known to have the largest Tiger population in the world, India has certainly not let Jim Corbett down. The Tiger featured prominently in ancient mythology and folk fore and continues to be depicted in modern films and literature, appearing on many flags , coats of arms and mascots for sporting teams. On the Indian Sub-continent it mainly inhabits tall grasslands and riverine forests as well as the swamp forests of the Sundarbans.



Since Tigers are mostly associated with the forest habitats, the only way to catch a glimpse of these charismatic creatures in the wild is on a Tiger Safari in India and every wildlife lover across the globe is agog to fulfill this dream. This is on every wildlife lovers bucket list. An encounter with these wild beauties is a mega treat to the eyes. This is a moment wherein two completely contradictory emotions arise in an individual as he/she is flabbergasted and get the jitters too, the moment the majestic animal appears from the wild. Ashlan Gorse Cousteau once said, “There is nothing like the thrill of walking through the jungle looking for a Tiger and knowing they could be watching you already”.

 

India is the prime destination in the world for Tiger spotting because of the dense population of Tigers and also because in India other important aspects of wildlife that includes bird watching, butterfly sighting and being aware of our diverse flora and fauna and learning ways to conserve them are taken care of. Hence , its best to come to India for the best Tiger Safari Tour.
